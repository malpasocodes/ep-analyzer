# Real Institutional Examples: Evidence for Each Critique Problem

*A companion to [EARNINGS_PREMIUM_CRITIQUE.md](EARNINGS_PREMIUM_CRITIQUE.md), providing named institutions and specific numbers from our dataset of 6,429 institutions.*

## Problem 1: The Geographic Fallacy — Punishing Place, Not Quality

The critique argues that statewide benchmarks crush enormous county-level variation into a single number. Here are the institutions that prove it.

### Institutions That Fail Statewide but Beat Their Local Benchmark

These institutions produce graduates who out-earn local high school graduates — but are classified as failures because their state threshold is inflated by urban metros.

| Institution | State | County | Earnings | State Threshold | County HS Earnings | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Denmark Technical College** | SC | Bamberg County | $25,351 | $30,156 | $21,980 | Fails state by $4,805 but beats county by $3,371 |
| **Sitting Bull College** | ND | Sioux County | $28,488 | $34,203 | $28,194 | Fails state by $5,715 but beats county by $294 |
| **Central Wyoming College** | WY | Fremont County | $34,402 | $36,480 | $33,011 | Fails state by $2,078 but beats county by $1,391 |
| **Southside Virginia Community College** | VA | Brunswick County | $32,371 | $33,043 | $30,526 | Fails state by $672 but beats county by $1,845 |
| **Mountain Empire Community College** | VA | Wise County | $32,622 | $33,043 | $31,003 | Fails state by $421 but beats county by $1,619 |
| **Danville Community College** | VA | Danville city | $31,664 | $33,043 | $31,337 | Fails state by $1,379 but beats county by $327 |

**Denmark Technical College** is the starkest example. It's a public HBCU in rural Bamberg County, South Carolina, where high school graduates earn $21,980. Its graduates earn $25,351 — **15% more** than local HS grads. By any local standard, it is adding clear economic value. But the statewide threshold is $30,156, inflated by Charleston and Columbia metro earnings. Denmark Tech fails — not because it's a bad school, but because it's in a poor county.

Three **Virginia community colleges** — Southside Virginia, Mountain Empire, and Danville — all serve Appalachian and Southside Virginia communities where local HS earnings are well below the state threshold. All three beat their county benchmarks. All three fail the state test.

### The Masking Problem: Institutions That Pass Statewide but Fail Locally

The geographic distortion cuts both ways. **945 institutions** pass the statewide threshold while producing graduates who earn less than local high school graduates. These are false passes — institutions in high-cost counties that clear a low bar.

| Institution | State | County | Earnings | State Threshold | County HS Earnings | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Ilisagvik College** | AK | North Slope Borough | $39,541 | $35,457 | $88,493 | Passes state by $4,084 but fails county by $48,952 |
| **University of New Mexico-Los Alamos Campus** | NM | Los Alamos County | $44,792 | $27,836 | $75,434 | Passes state by $16,956 but fails county by $30,642 |
| **Northwest Kansas Technical College** | KS | Sherman County | $38,616 | $30,782 | $57,649 | Passes state by $7,834 but fails county by $19,033 |
| **Colorado Northwestern Community College** | CO | Rio Blanco County | $43,526 | $35,571 | $59,770 | Passes state by $7,955 but fails county by $16,244 |
| **New York College of Health Professions** | NY | Nassau County | $31,601 | $30,793 | $46,903 | Passes state by $808 but fails county by $15,302 |

**Ilisagvik College** in Alaska's North Slope Borough illustrates the absurdity. Local HS graduates earn $88,493 — driven by oil industry wages. Ilisagvik's graduates earn $39,541, barely half the local HS rate. Under any local standard, this is a concerning outcome. But Alaska's statewide threshold is $35,457, and Ilisagvik passes.

This is not a small problem. The **945 false passes outnumber the 11 false failures by 86-to-1**. The statewide benchmark's dominant failure mode is not punishing good schools — it's protecting mediocre ones in wealthy areas.

### Same-State Contrasts

In **Alabama**, Fortis College in Mobile County ($26,652 earnings, where county HS earnings are $37,258) *fails* the state test by $1,184. Meanwhile, J.F. Drake State in Madison County ($28,281 earnings, where county HS earnings are $37,295) *passes* by $445. The $1,629 earnings difference between them is dwarfed by identical local labor markets — their divergent outcomes are driven entirely by which side of the threshold they land on.

## Problem 2: The Ecological Fallacy — Comparing Apples to Averages

The critique argues that comparing small program cohorts against population-level benchmarks is a textbook ecological fallacy. These examples show how small the "cohorts" can be.

### Institutions with Minimal Completions Near the Threshold

| Institution | State | Completions | Earnings | Threshold | Margin | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Oklahoma Technical College** | OK | 1 | $28,954 | $29,810 | -2.9% | FAILS |
| **Urban College of Boston** | MA | 1 | $35,472 | $35,438 | +0.1% | PASSES |
| **Messenger College** | TX | 2 | $29,463 | $31,171 | -5.5% | FAILS |
| **West Virginia Junior College-UCI** | PA | 2 | $31,912 | $31,727 | +0.6% | PASSES |
| **Remington College-North Houston** | TX | 5 | $31,349 | $31,171 | +0.6% | PASSES |
| **Midwest Institute** | MO | 7 | $30,414 | $30,156 | +0.9% | PASSES |
| **WellSpring School of Allied Health** | KS | 8 | $29,839 | $30,782 | -3.1% | FAILS |

**Oklahoma Technical College** has **1 completer**. That single person's earnings determine the institution's pass/fail outcome — compared against a benchmark derived from millions of Oklahoma workers. This is not a statistical test. It is a coin flip dressed up as accountability.

**Urban College of Boston** also has **1 completer** and passes by $34 — a margin of 0.1%. The entire institution's federal aid eligibility rests on one person's salary being $34 above a population-derived threshold.

**1,170 institutions** that fail the statewide test have 50 or fewer total completions. The EP test applies the same binary judgment to an institution with 1 completer as to one with 10,000.

## Problem 3: Simpson's Paradox — Aggregation Hides Reality

The critique argues that statewide aggregation can reverse the true picture. Here's where it happens.

### Within-State County Earnings Variation

The statewide threshold assumes economic homogeneity. The data says otherwise:

| State | County Earnings Range | Ratio |
|---|---|---|
| **Texas** | $15,238 – $84,750 | 5.6x |
| **Alaska** | $26,667 – $88,493 | 3.3x |
| **New Mexico** | $18,542 – $75,434 | 4.1x |
| **Louisiana** | $19,713 – $65,259 | 3.3x |
| **Kansas** | $19,833 – $59,145 | 3.0x |

Texas has a **$69,512 range** in county HS earnings — yet applies a single $31,171 threshold to every institution. An institution in Hidalgo County (HS earnings: $29,320) faces the same bar as an institution in Midland County (HS earnings above $80,000). The statewide threshold is meaningless for both: too high for one, too low for the other.

### Institutions That Beat Every Local Standard but Fail the State

| Institution | State | County | Earnings vs County HS | State Threshold | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Denmark Technical College** | SC | Bamberg County | +15% above county | $30,156 | FAILS |
| **South Texas Vocational Technical Institute-Weslaco** | TX | Hidalgo County | +6% above county | $31,171 | FAILS |
| **Southside Virginia Community College** | VA | Brunswick County | +6% above county | $33,043 | FAILS |
| **Mountain Empire Community College** | VA | Wise County | +5% above county | $33,043 | FAILS |
| **Central Wyoming College** | WY | Fremont County | +4% above county | $36,480 | FAILS |

Every one of these institutions demonstrates a Simpson's paradox reversal: *at the local level*, they outperform their benchmark. *At the aggregate level*, they fail.

### Sector Divergence Within States

When disaggregated by sector, pass rates tell a very different story:

| State | Public Fail Rate | For-Profit Fail Rate |
|---|---|---|
| **Virginia** | 10% | 49% |
| **Colorado** | 0% | 40% |
| **Texas** | 0% | 34% |
| **Pennsylvania** | 0% | 31% |

In **Texas**, zero public institutions fail but 34% of for-profits do. In **Virginia**, 10% of public institutions fail vs 49% of for-profits. The aggregate statewide pass rate masks completely different realities by sector. A policymaker looking at aggregate pass rates would miss this entirely.

## Problem 4: Measurement Error — The Threshold Is a Cliff, Not a Line

### Late Bloomers: Fail at 6 Years, Pass at 10

**516 institutions** fail the earnings test at 6 years post-enrollment but pass at 10 years. These are not failing programs — they are programs whose graduates have normal career trajectories.

| Institution | State | P6 Earnings | P10 Earnings | Threshold | P6→P10 Growth |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **University of Arkansas Grantham** | AR | $26,870 | $63,496 | $28,502 | +$36,626 (+136%) |
| **Moreno Valley College** | CA | $31,830 | $52,133 | $32,476 | +$20,303 (+64%) |
| **Angeles College** | CA | $26,732 | $49,108 | $32,476 | +$22,376 (+84%) |
| **Indiana Institute of Technology** | IN | $29,717 | $47,327 | $31,316 | +$17,610 (+59%) |
| **Laguna College of Art and Design** | CA | $31,987 | $47,867 | $32,476 | +$15,880 (+50%) |
| **Clark Atlanta University** | GA | $28,097 | $42,712 | $29,609 | +$14,615 (+52%) |
| **University of Maine at Farmington** | ME | $31,513 | $44,433 | $32,311 | +$12,920 (+41%) |
| **Grambling State University** | LA | $25,589 | $41,109 | $28,996 | +$15,520 (+61%) |

**University of Arkansas Grantham** graduates earn $26,870 at 6 years — failing by $1,632. By year 10, they earn $63,496 — **passing by $34,994**. Their earnings more than double. Under an EP test that catches them at year 6, they would lose federal aid despite producing graduates with strong career trajectories.

**Clark Atlanta University** and **Grambling State University** — both HBCUs — fail at 6 years but pass comfortably at 10 years. An earnings snapshot penalizes the timing of their graduates' career growth, not the quality of their education.

Across all institutions with both P6 and P10 data, the **median earnings growth is $5,018 (15.0%)**. **1,637 institutions** show P6-to-P10 growth exceeding 20%. An EP test locked to a single time window ignores this trajectory entirely.

### The Threshold Cliff: ±0.1% Determines Federal Aid Eligibility

**377 institutions** sit within ±5% of their state threshold. The narrowest margins are vanishingly small:

| Institution | State | Earnings | Threshold | Margin | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **CUNY Stella and Charles Guttman Community College** | NY | $30,787 | $30,793 | -0.02% | FAILS |
| **Penn Commercial Business/Technical School** | PA | $31,736 | $31,727 | +0.03% | PASSES |
| **North-West College-Van Nuys** | CA | $32,488 | $32,476 | +0.04% | PASSES |
| **Roanoke-Chowan Community College** | NC | $29,324 | $29,344 | -0.07% | FAILS |
| **East Arkansas Community College** | AR | $28,471 | $28,502 | -0.11% | FAILS |
| **Cuyamaca College** | CA | $32,435 | $32,476 | -0.13% | FAILS |
| **Mountwest Community and Technical College** | WV | $28,951 | $28,996 | -0.16% | FAILS |

**CUNY Stella and Charles Guttman Community College** — a public institution in New York City — fails by **$6**. Not $6,000. Six dollars. One student taking a slightly higher-paying shift, one rounding difference in the data, and the outcome flips. There is no statistical framework in which a $6 difference on a ~$30,000 measure constitutes a meaningful signal.

**Roanoke-Chowan Community College** in rural North Carolina fails by $20. It also *passes* the county-level benchmark (county HS earnings: $29,293). It is simultaneously a local success and a statewide failure — over twenty dollars.

## Problem 5: What the Metric Doesn't Measure

### Sector Tells the Story the Metric Misses

Fail rates by sector in selected states:

**California:**
- Public 4-year: **0% fail** (0/48), median earnings $62,530
- Public 2-year: **2% fail** (2/99), median earnings $42,195
- For-profit 4-year: **9% fail** (3/34), median earnings $50,428

**Texas:**
- Public 4-year: **0% fail** (0/55), median earnings $49,652
- Public 2-year: **0% fail** (0/39), median earnings $38,806
- For-profit 2-year: **57% fail** (4/7), median earnings $30,187

**Florida:**
- Public 4-year: **0% fail** (0/41), median earnings $41,728
- For-profit 2-year: **43% fail** (6/14), median earnings $31,210
- For-profit 4-year: **4% fail** (1/23), median earnings $39,080

The EP test's statewide threshold doesn't distinguish between sectors, missions, or student populations. For-profit 2-year institutions fail at dramatically higher rates not because the threshold is too high, but because the metric treats a for-profit cosmetology school and a public research university as equivalent enterprises.

Removing their federal aid eligibility would eliminate higher education access for communities that have no alternative.

These are not institutions that failed their students. They are institutions whose students face structural barriers that no earnings metric can capture.
